## GROMACS Tutorial

**Step Four: Analysis**

The bar module of GROMACS makes the calculation of ΔGAB very simple. Simply collect all of the md*.xvg files that were produced by mdrun (one for each value of λ) in the working directory and run gmx bar:

```
gmx bar -f Lambda_*/Production_MD/md*.xvg -o -oi
```

The value of ΔGAB I obtained is 17.80 ± 0.20 kJ mol<sup>-1</sup>, or 4.25 ± 0.05 kcal mol<sup>-1</sup>. Since the process I conducted for this demonstration was the transformation of ethanol into a dummy molecule, the reverse process (the introduction of uncharged ethanol into water, thus the actual hydration free energy of the process) corresponds to a ΔGAB of -4.25 ± 0.05 kcal mol<sup>-1</sup> (assuming reversibility). This value differs slightly from the [experimental value](https://pubs.acs.org/doi/10.1021/ct050164b) of -5.01 kcal mol<sup>-1</sup> and the value of -5.34 ± 0.12 kcal mol<sup>-1</sup>[previously reported with CHARMM22](https://dx.doi.org/10.1021/ct700100a). Deviations are likely due to the fact that the topology used here came from the CGenFF parameter set, rather than the ethanol model compound optimized for the CHARMM protein force field; there are minor differences in partial charge assignments and Lennard-Jones parameters of the constitutent atom types.

As in the example of [methane in water](../methane_water/index.html), one can plot the output from the BAR program to see how the free energy differences between neighboring λ windows resulted in the final output. We will not plot them in this tutorial.
